Mohamed Salah is an Egyptian footballer and one of the world's best players. Born on June 15, 1992, in Nagrig, Egypt, he started his career at Al Mokawloon before moving to Basel. He later played for Chelsea, Fiorentina, and Roma, where he gained fame. In 2017, he joined Liverpool and won major titles like the Champions League in 2019 and the Premier League in 2020. Known for his speed and goal-scoring ability, Salah is also admired for his humility and charity work in Egypt.

Roger Federer is a Swiss tennis legend, widely regarded as one of the greatest players in history. Born in 1981, he won 20 Grand Slam titles, including Wimbledon and the US Open. Known for his elegance, skill, and sportsmanship, Federer dominated tennis for decades. He retired in 2022, leaving a lasting legacy in the sport.

Michael Phelps is an American swimmer and the most decorated Olympian in history, with 23 Olympic gold medals. Born in 1985, he dominated swimming with his speed and endurance. Phelps set multiple world records and won medals in four Olympic Games. His achievements made him a legend in the sport.
